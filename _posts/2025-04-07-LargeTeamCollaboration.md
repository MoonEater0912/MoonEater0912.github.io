This is the research I worked on as a research assistant at Aalto University, with Duong Nguyen and Barbara Keller. This work is accpeted to CHI 2026 in Barcelona.

The full paper can be seen [here](/files/chi26-930.pdf). The core idea is that, large-team collaboration (which is increasingly popular across all disciplines) not only serves as a career accelerator, but also reproduces inequality, thus contributing to the disadvantageous positions of women in academia.

We are currently developing another relevant project, arguing that homophily can, even without explicit, intentional exclusion, prevent the minority from being recruited into large teams. The preliminary results can be seen [here](https://mooneater0912.github.io/post/2026/01/26/TeamSizeHomophily).

I developed a Python Package implementing the core causal identification technique used in this study (Synthetic DID), which can be seen [here](https://github.com/MoonEater0912/SyntheticDID?tab=readme-ov-file).
