About Me

Get to know me interactively - Chat with my “digital twin”!

I am Zeyuan Chen (陈 泽远). I'm from a small coastal county in southeastern mainland China. After one year of studying mathematics, I completed a double bachelor's degree in Philosophy and Sociology at Beijing Normal University. I am currently a 2nd-year master student in Social Sciences at Uppsala University (graduating in 2026).

I am interested in computational approaches to socio-cultural analysis - particularly in developing and using interpretable NLP and network analysis methods to discover and understand how inequality structurally manifests in specific context, and how it is reproduced through certain cultural and institutional processes. I am currently seeking PhD opportunities in related topics (Fall 2026 or later). My CV can be found here.

My research interests include:

  • Computational social science, especially interpretable and context-aware NLP and network analysis
  • Mechanism design and network simulation in studies of knowledge and politics
  • Geometric data analysis in studies of culture and education
  • Inequality, stratification, and social conflict
  • Developing relevant research toolkits and data pipelines

Beyond research, I love reading, playing video games, city walking, and learning languages. My favorite food includes iced rice, Chaoshan beef hotpot, and luosifen.
